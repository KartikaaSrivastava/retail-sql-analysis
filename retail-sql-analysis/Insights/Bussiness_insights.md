## Revenue Analysis

### Monthly Revenue Trends
- Revenue shows clear seasonality, with consistent peaks in **March–April** and **October–November**.
- **February** and **September** are recurring low-revenue months.
- Revenue volatility is higher in 2020–2021, while 2022–2024 shows more stable performance.

**Insight:** Sales are strongly seasonal and increasingly predictable in recent years.  

---

### Revenue by Product Category
- **Beauty** and **Clothing** together contribute the majority of total revenue.
- **Accessories** form a mid-tier category, while **Footwear** and **Jewelry** contribute less.
- The **“Unknown”** category contributes negligible revenue and reflects data quality issues.

**Insight:** Revenue is concentrated in a few core categories.  

---

### Revenue by Product Type (Seasonality)
- **Seasonal** products generate the highest revenue, followed by **Formal** products.
- **Casual** products contribute the least revenue.

**Insight:** Customers respond more strongly to seasonal and occasion-based products.  

---

### Profitability by Category
- **Clothing** has the highest gross profit and margin.
- **Jewelry** shows high margins but lower sales volume.
- **Beauty** generates high revenue but operates at comparatively lower margins.

**Insight:** High revenue does not always translate to high profitability.  

---

### Top Revenue-Generating Products
- A small set of products contributes disproportionately to total revenue.
- Most top-performing products belong to Beauty and Clothing categories.

**Insight:** Revenue is concentrated among a limited number of SKUs.  

## Promotional Analysis

### Discount vs Non-Discount Performance
- Discounted and non-discounted transactions show **similar average units sold**.
- Discounted orders have a **slightly higher average order value**, but the difference is marginal.

**Insight:** Discounts do not significantly increase purchase volume and provide limited uplift in order value.  
---

### Promotion-wise Revenue Performance
- All promotions generate comparable revenue levels.
- **PROMO003 and PROMO004** generate the highest revenue with relatively controlled discount costs.
- No single promotion dramatically outperforms others.

**Insight:** Promotional effectiveness varies, but higher discounts do not necessarily translate into higher revenue.  
---

### Seasonal Discount Trends
- Discount usage spikes consistently during **April** and **November** across years.
- Lower discount activity is observed in early-year and late-summer months.

**Insight:** Discounts are strongly aligned with seasonal and festive periods rather than being evenly distributed.
